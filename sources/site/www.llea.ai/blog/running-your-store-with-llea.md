A common worry before installing llea.ai: _"will this become another dashboard I'm obliged to babysit?"_. Fair concern — most marketing tools quietly add 30 minutes a day to someone's calendar without admitting it. This post is the honest walkthrough of how a real marketing team uses llea.ai across the week, and why the time investment is small by design.

## The 10-minute morning routine

The first thing you'll do each day is open **Today's queue** — a ranked list of customers who crossed the intent threshold overnight or in the last few hours. Each row shows who they are, what they're interested in, and a suggested next action.

## The 30-minute weekly planning session

Once a week — most teams make it Monday morning — you spend ~30 minutes in the **Trends** view. That's where you see:

- Which products and categories are generating the most organic intent this week
- How intent is trending week-over-week — is your audience warming up or cooling off?
- Which campaigns from last week actually moved the needle (and which were noise)
- Which segments are growing and which are aging out

That session is where you decide the campaign focus for the week — what to push, what to restock, what to leave alone. It replaces the "what should we send this week?" guesswork meeting most teams already have.

## Setting up alerts (so you barely need to log in)

If even 10 minutes a day feels like too much, set alerts and skip most of the dashboard time. Alerts can fire on:

- A customer crossing a specific intent threshold (e.g. score > 85)
- A high-LTV repeat customer returning to your site
- An unusual intent spike on a specific product or collection
- A segment shrinking faster than expected (early churn signal)

Alerts route to email, Slack, or webhook. Most teams set 2–3 alerts that matter and ignore the rest of the dashboard until Monday.

## Roles and team access

Multiple team members can access the same llea.ai workspace, each with the right level of access:

| Role | Can do |
| --- | --- |
| Owner | Everything — billing, integrations, user management |
| Marketer | Build segments, approve campaigns, edit alerts |
| Analyst | Read-only on dashboards and exports |
| Operator | Approve / dismiss daily-queue items only |

We default to least privilege. A teammate scanning the morning queue doesn't need access to your Shopify billing or your integration secrets, and the role system reflects that.

## Exporting and acting on the data

Every segment is one click away from being a Klaviyo audience, a WhatsApp broadcast list, a Shopify customer tag, or a CSV download for your retargeting tool. You don't have to leave llea.ai to action what you find — but you also don't have to send messages through llea.ai. We hand off to the tools you already use, with the audience already shaped.

Common pattern: build a "ready to buy" segment in llea.ai, sync it to Klaviyo, attach your existing abandoned-cart Flow, and let the Flow only fire on shoppers who actually have intent. Same Flow you've had for a year, just landing on a sharper list.

## Filtering and segmentation

Every dashboard view supports filtering by:

- Product or collection interest (intent on a specific SKU, or category-level)
- Geography (country, region — useful for shipping-bound promotions)
- Customer recency (first-time vs returning vs lapsed)
- Order history (LTV bucket, average order value, frequency)
- Channel of first acquisition (organic, paid, email, referral)

Filters stack. "Show me first-time visitors from California with intent on the new Linen collection" is a single dashboard query, not an export-and-pivot exercise.

## What you don't need

- **You don't need a data analyst.** Every chart is read directly. There's no SQL surface for end users; the answers come pre-formatted.
- **You don't need a technical background.** If you can use Klaviyo or Shopify Admin, you can run llea.ai.
- **You don't need to be in the dashboard all day.** Alerts + a 10-minute morning routine is the design target.

## What "good" looks like after a month

By week four, most teams have a steady rhythm: morning queue, weekly planning, one or two automated Flows powered by llea.ai segments. The dashboard tells you what to do next, and the actioning happens in the tools you already had. The point is to remove "what should we send today?" from your mental load — not add a new tool to learn.
