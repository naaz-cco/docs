Llea reserves WhatsApp for the shoppers who mean business — those with an intent score of 90 or above. At that threshold, a direct offer via WhatsApp converts within a 24–48 hour window, making it the highest-ROI channel in your stack. Llea supports four WhatsApp Business providers: **Wati**, **AiSensy**, **Interakt**, and **Gallabox**. Each connects through Shopify Flow (via the provider's native Flow connector or an HTTP webhook, depending on what your provider exposes), so your existing templates and opt-in lists stay exactly where they are.

## When Llea Triggers a WhatsApp Message

Llea fires a Shopify Flow event the moment a shopper's intent score crosses your configured threshold. The default threshold is **90**, placing these shoppers in the highest-intent band where direct, time-sensitive offers perform best. Once triggered, the conversion window is 24–48 hours — act fast, and the message lands when purchase intent is at its peak.

<Info>
  You can adjust the intent score threshold in your Llea dashboard under **Settings → Channel Routing**. The default of 90 is the recommended starting point for WhatsApp.
</Info>

## Connect Your WhatsApp Provider

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Wati">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Enable the Wati Shopify connector">
        In your Wati dashboard, go to **Integrations → Shopify** and enable the Shopify app connector. Install the Wati app from the Shopify App Store if you have not done so already. Wati registers a native Shopify Flow action once the connector is active.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Create a Shopify Flow Automation on the Llea event">
        In Shopify admin, open **Flow** and create a new Automation. Set the trigger to the Llea **"High Intent Customer"** event. Add a condition: `intent_score is greater than or equal to 90` (or your custom threshold). This ensures only your top-intent shoppers enter the flow.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Add the Wati Send WhatsApp Message action">
        Under the condition step, add a **Wati: Send WhatsApp Message** action. Select the approved message template you want to send. Wati will only allow templates that have already passed WhatsApp Business API approval — see the warning below if you have not submitted yours yet.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Map Llea event properties to your template variables">
        In the Wati action panel, map the Llea event properties to your template placeholders. Use the values listed in the [Message Template Variables](#message-template-variables) section below to populate product name, customer name, discount code, and segment fields.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="AiSensy / Interakt / Gallabox">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Install your provider's Shopify app">
        Find your provider's app in the Shopify App Store (search for AiSensy, Interakt, or Gallabox) and install it. In your provider's dashboard, enable the Shopify integration so that a Flow connector or webhook endpoint becomes available.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Create a Shopify Flow Automation on the Llea event">
        In Shopify admin, open **Flow** and create a new Automation. Set the trigger to the Llea **"High Intent Customer"** event. Add a condition: `intent_score is greater than or equal to 90`. This filters the flow to your highest-intent shoppers only.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Add your provider's message action or Send HTTP Request">
        If your provider exposes a native Flow action (such as **AiSensy: Send WhatsApp** or **Gallabox: Send Message**), add that action and select your approved template. If your provider does not yet have a native connector, add a **Send HTTP Request** action instead — paste in your provider's API endpoint, include your API key in the headers, and pass the template ID and recipient number in the request body.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Message Template Variables

Llea passes the following properties on every **"High Intent Customer"** Flow event. Reference these in your WhatsApp template variables to send a fully personalised message without any manual segmentation.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Recommended Product" icon="tag">
    `{{ trigger.recommended_product_title }}`

    The specific product the shopper engaged with most during their session — the item they are most likely to buy.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Customer First Name" icon="user">
    `{{ customer.first_name }}`

    The shopper's first name pulled directly from their Shopify customer record.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Behavioural Segment" icon="chart-bar">
    `{{ trigger.customer_segment }}`

    One of four segments: `impulse_buyer`, `researcher`, `hesitant_explorer`, or `loyalist`. Use this to switch template tone without building separate flows.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Personalised Discount Code" icon="ticket">
    `{{ trigger.personalised_discount_code }}`

    A single-use, named discount code generated for this shopper (for example, `SARAH-10`). Each code is unique and expires after redemption.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Cooldown and Opt-Out Handling

Llea enforces a **48-hour cooldown** after every WhatsApp send. If a shopper does not respond within that window, Llea will not re-trigger a WhatsApp message for the same shopper until the cooldown expires — regardless of whether their intent score rises again. This prevents the brand-damaging experience of sending the same person three messages in a week.

Opt-outs are honoured automatically and propagate across every channel. When a shopper opts out of WhatsApp, Llea suppresses them from Llea-triggered SMS and email flows as well. You do not need to manage cross-channel suppression lists manually.

<Warning>
  WhatsApp Business messaging requires approved message templates. Submit your templates for approval in your WhatsApp provider dashboard before going live. Llea fires the Flow event regardless — but your provider will reject the send if the template is not yet approved.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  Segment-aware copy performs best on WhatsApp. Impulse buyers respond to urgency ("Only 3 left — grab yours now"), while Researchers want product detail and reassurance ("Here's why 400 customers chose this"). Use `{{ trigger.customer_segment }}` in a Flow condition to branch into two template variants without doubling your setup effort.
</Tip>
