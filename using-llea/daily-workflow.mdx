Most marketing teams run Llea in about 10 minutes every weekday morning, plus one 30-minute planning session on Mondays. That's the full time commitment — by design. Llea surfaces the decisions that matter and hands off to the tools you already use, so you spend your time acting on intelligence rather than hunting for it.

## The 10-Minute Morning Routine

Every morning, open **Today's queue** — a ranked list of customers who crossed the intent threshold since your last review. Each row tells you who the customer is, what they're interested in, and what Llea recommends you do next. Scan the list, approve the actions you agree with, and close the tab.

Approving an action fires your existing infrastructure — your Klaviyo Flow, your WhatsApp template, or your retargeting export. You don't build anything new; Llea shapes the audience and your existing tools do the sending.

### Example Queue

| Customer | Intent Score | Product Interest | Suggested Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Olivia M. | 92 | Linen Shirt · Sand | WhatsApp · 10% off |
| Mason H. | 88 | Cargo Trousers · Olive | Email · restock note |
| Charlotte K. | 74 | Knit Cardigan · Cream | Suppress — bought 2d ago |
| Ethan W. | 71 | Oxford Shirt · Pinpoint | WhatsApp · soft nudge |

For each entry you can **approve** the recommended action, **edit** it (swap the template or change the channel), or **skip** it entirely. The whole list rarely takes more than ten minutes, including the coffee.

<Tip>
  The **Today's queue** view also respects your dashboard filters — narrow by product, geography, or customer recency to focus on the segment that matters most to you that day.
</Tip>

## The 30-Minute Weekly Planning Session

Once a week — most teams anchor this to Monday morning — spend 30 minutes in the **Trends** view. This is where you make campaign decisions for the week ahead rather than guessing.

Review these four areas each Monday:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Product and Category Intent">
    Check which products and categories are generating the most organic intent this week. High-intent product clusters tell you what to feature in campaigns before you commit send budget.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Week-Over-Week Intent Trend">
    Look at whether overall intent is warming up or cooling off compared to last week. A rising trend is a signal to push harder; a dip prompts you to check whether a campaign or a seasonal shift is driving it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Last Week's Campaign Performance">
    See which campaigns from the previous week moved the needle on intent scores and which were noise. Use this to cut what didn't work and double down on what did.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Segment Growth and Ageing">
    Identify which segments are growing — new high-intent customers entering — and which are ageing out. Segments that shrink fast are early churn signals worth acting on.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  The Trends view is also where you decide the week's campaign focus, replacing the "what should we send?" guesswork meeting. Walk out of your Monday session with a clear send plan rather than a debate.
</Tip>

The total weekly commitment is roughly 80 minutes: a 10-minute queue review each weekday (10 min × 5) plus the 30-minute Monday planning session. Most teams find the actual number is lower because alerts reduce the days when the queue needs any hands-on attention at all.

## What "Good" Looks Like After One Month

By week four, expect a steady rhythm with these markers in place:

<Accordion title="Steady morning queue habit">
  The queue review takes under five minutes most days because the intent threshold is calibrated to your store's typical buyer behaviour. You're approving two or three actions, not scanning fifty rows.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Automated Flows running on intent-shaped lists">
  One or two Klaviyo Flows — abandoned-cart, browse-abandonment, or win-back — are connected to Llea segments and fire automatically. The same Flow you've had for a year now lands on shoppers who actually have intent, not everyone who visited.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Campaign decisions anchored to data">
  The Monday Trends session replaces the "what should we send this week?" meeting. Your team arrives at send decisions faster and with less debate because the intent data gives you a concrete starting point.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Alerts covering the gaps">
  Two or three alerts are live — at minimum a score threshold alert and a high-LTV customer return alert — so nothing urgent slips through on the days when you close the tab in three minutes flat.
</Accordion>

<Info>
  You don't need a data analyst or a technical background to reach this rhythm. If you can use Klaviyo or Shopify Admin, you already have everything you need to run Llea day-to-day.
</Info>
